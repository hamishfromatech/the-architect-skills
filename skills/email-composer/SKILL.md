# Email Composer Skill

Compose professional business emails with proper structure, tone, and formatting.

## Capabilities

- Compose formal, semi-formal, and casual business emails
- Generate email templates for common scenarios
- Format emails with proper greetings, body structure, and sign-offs
- Adapt tone based on recipient relationship and context

## Process

1. **Understand Context**: Identify the email purpose, recipient relationship, and desired tone
2. **Structure the Email**: Create subject line, greeting, body, and sign-off
3. **Apply Tone**: Adjust formality level (formal/semi-formal/casual)
4. **Review**: Ensure clarity, conciseness, and professionalism

## Email Structure Template

```
Subject: [Clear, action-oriented subject line]

[Salutation appropriate to relationship]

[Opening: State purpose clearly]

[Body: Elaborate with necessary details]

[Closing: Call to action or next steps]

[Sign-off appropriate to tone]

[Signature block]
```

## Tone Guidelines

### Formal
- Use: Dear [Title] [Last Name], Sincerely
- For: Executives, clients, first-time contacts, sensitive matters
- Avoid: Contractions, informal expressions

### Semi-Formal
- Use: Dear [First Name], Best regards
- For: Regular clients, colleagues, established relationships
- Style: Professional but approachable

### Casual
- Use: Hi [First Name], Thanks/Best
- For: Team members, close colleagues
- Style: Friendly, efficient

## Common Email Types

| Type | Purpose | Key Elements |
|------|---------|--------------|
| Introduction | Establish new contact | Context, purpose, call to action |
| Request | Ask for information/action | Clear request, deadline, reason |
| Follow-up | Check status/progress | Reference, status check, next steps |
| Proposal | Present ideas/offers | Value proposition, details, CTA |
| Apology | Address mistakes | Acknowledge, apologize, solution |
| Thank You | Express gratitude | Specific thanks, impact, forward look |
